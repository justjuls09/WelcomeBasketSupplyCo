Usability goal: A first‑time user should complete the core task without explanation.

## User Personas

### 1. Sarah - No Tech Experience (Novice)
- **Goal**: Order a welcome kit without feeling overwhelmed or making mistakes
- **Frustration**: Complex forms, too many options, unclear instructions, error messages that don't explain what went wrong

### 2. Marcus - Basic Tech Skills (Beginner)
- **Goal**: Quickly complete the order on mobile and move on
- **Frustration**: Buttons that are too small, having to scroll too much, unexpected navigation changes, lengthy confirmation processes

### 3. Elena - Comfortable with Web Apps (Intermediate)
- **Goal**: Customize her welcome kit options and save preferences for future orders
- **Frustration**: Limited customization options, no way to save her profile, having to re-enter information repeatedly, inability to modify orders

### 4. James - Tech-Savvy (Advanced)
- **Goal**: Complete the order efficiently with keyboard shortcuts and auto-fill capabilities
- **Frustration**: Slow app performance, redundant steps, inability to batch order or use keyboard navigation, no API access for automation

### 5. Priya - Expert User (Power User)
- **Goal**: Integrate the ordering system with her team's workflow and access detailed analytics
- **Frustration**: No export options, inability to manage bulk orders, lack of developer documentation, no webhook support for automated workflows

## Testing Results
"I just want to order this welcome kit quickly on my phone during my lunch break..."

- What would confuse me:

- Unclear button labels - If I see a button that says "Submit" or "Proceed," I'm not always sure what happens next. Does it charge my card? Save my info? Why are there so many buttons?

- Where do I start? - If the app doesn't clearly show me the first step (like a welcome message or progress indicator), I'll feel lost. "Do I click here? Or here?"

- Hidden information - Dropdown menus or collapsed sections confuse me. I might miss important options because I don't realize they're there. I'd prefer to see what I'm choosing from.

- Technical language - Terms like "payload," "API," "configure defaults," or even just "select options" - I need simpler words. Just tell me what I'm doing.

- Error messages - If something goes wrong and I see "Invalid input: field_456 validation failed," I have no idea what I did wrong. What field? What should I do?

- What would frustrate me:

- Mobile doesn't work well - Buttons are too small, text is hard to read, or I have to scroll horizontally. I'll just close the app and order elsewhere.

- Losing my work - If I go back or accidentally refresh, and everything disappears, I'll be annoyed. Did I already fill this out?

- Too many steps - More than 5-6 screens to complete an order feels excessive. I'm not buying a house; I just want a kit.

- Confirmation anxiety - No clear review screen before I finalize. I want to see everything I selected: "2x t-shirts, size medium, blue color" before I click "Order."

- No progress indication - If I don't see "Step 1 of 4" or a progress bar, I don't know how close I am to finishing. Am I halfway done or almost done?

- Slow load times or freezing - If the app takes 3+ seconds to respond when I click something, I think it's broken and try clicking again, which makes things worse.

- No way to go back - If I can only move forward and can't edit my choices, I feel trapped. Let me fix my mistake!

- Unexpected navigation - Like if clicking a logo takes me back to the home page and I lose my cart. That's not intuitive.
